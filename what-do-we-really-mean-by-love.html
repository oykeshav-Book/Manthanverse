What Do We Really Mean by Love?

A conversation between Keshav and Gopi exploring the nature of love, attachment, devotion, and the journey from ordinary affection to divine love.

Characters: Keshav • Gopi

Keshav: Gopi, have you ever been in love? 🙂

Gopi: Ohho... you're really eager to know, aren't you? Do you even know what love is?

Keshav: No, that's exactly why I'm asking. People talk so much about love, but everyone seems to have a different definition of it. What's yours? 😄

Gopi: Love cannot really be defined. That's precisely why it is love.

Keshav: Really? Then how do people recognize that what they're feeling is love and not something else?

Gopi: Why do you think you're in love in the first place?

Keshav: Maybe because the heart has a habit of giving a name to everything that feels deep. 😄

But alright, let me ask the opposite question—if someone doesn't even realize that they're in love, can love still be present?

Gopi: They may call it something else, but if love is there, it cannot go unnoticed.

Keshav: So according to you, love cannot remain hidden? Meaning that the day it truly happens, whether a person accepts it or not, they will definitely know?

Gopi: Yes. Because love is experiential in nature. If there is no experience of it, then what kind of love is that?

Keshav: Yes, that sounds reasonable. If there is no experience at all, then it would remain nothing more than an imagination or an idea.

But then tell me, what does the experience of love actually feel like?

Gopi: It is difficult to confine love within words. But saints have described some of its characteristics—it is connected with truth, consciousness, and bliss; it grows with every passing moment; it is beyond selfish desire; it is extremely subtle and can only be directly experienced.

What the experience of love feels like depends on its stage and depth. But its movement is always in the direction of love itself.

Understand?

Keshav: I understand... or at least I'm trying to. 😄

You're saying that love is not an ordinary emotion. It contains awareness, bliss, and it keeps growing every moment.

But then I have a confusion...

If someone has desire, selfishness, and love all at the same time, is that love, or is it merely a movement toward love?

Gopi: First, one must see what the desire is for, toward whom it is directed, and what motivates it. Not every desire is the same.

If the desire is driven by one's own pleasure, possession, or the urge to obtain something, then it may be closer to attachment or rati.

And if the desire is for the well-being and happiness of the beloved, then it may be moving in the direction of love.

In its fullness, love is beyond desire and selfishness. Therefore, based on this alone, one cannot definitively say whether it is love or not.

However, if confusion, selfishness, and possessiveness are very strong, then it is possible that love has not yet fully manifested, and the person is still in some intermediate state between rati, attachment, and love.
