Do we really have free will

A conversation between Keshav and Nakul exploring one of philosophy's oldest questions: Are our choices truly free, or are they shaped by causes beyond our awareness?

Characters: Keshav • Nakul

Nakul

Keshav, can I ask you something?

People always say, "Work hard and create your own destiny."

But the same people also say...

"Whatever is meant to happen will happen."

I don't get it.

If everything is already destined, then what's the point of effort?

And if my efforts can truly change everything, then what exactly is destiny?

How can both be true at the same time?

Either destiny is real...

Or free will is.

How can both exist together? 🤔

Keshav

Hey Nakul...

What exactly do you mean by free will? I'm having a hard time connecting with the term.

Nakul

😂 That's exactly what I'm trying to figure out.

In simple words...

Free will means that the decisions I make are genuinely my own.

Like whether I choose tea or coffee...

Whether I study or scroll through my phone...

Are those choices really mine, or were they already decided?

That's the real question.

Keshav

Alright, it's starting to make sense.

But tell me something.

If we really have free will, why does everyone still follow the same basic rules?

It's not like we're completely independent.

We still have laws.

We can't just go around murdering people or do anything we want.

There are so many things we simply can't do.

Nakul

Hold on... you're mixing up two different things. 😄

Free will doesn't mean you can do absolutely anything.

It simply asks this:

Among the choices actually available to you... who is making the choice?

You?

Or was it already decided?

That's the real question.
