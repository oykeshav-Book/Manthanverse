Do we really have free will

A conversation between Keshav and Nakul exploring one of philosophy's oldest questions: Are our choices truly free, or are they shaped by causes beyond our awareness?

Characters: Keshav • Nakul

Nakul

Keshav, can I ask you something?

People always say, "Work hard and create your own destiny."

But the same people also say...

"Whatever is meant to happen will happen."

I don't get it.

If everything is already destined, then what's the point of effort?

And if my efforts can truly change everything, then what exactly is destiny?

How can both be true at the same time?

Either destiny is real...

Or free will is.

How can both exist together? 🤔

Keshav

Hey Nakul...

What exactly do you mean by free will? I'm having a hard time connecting with the term.

Nakul

😂 That's exactly what I'm trying to figure out.

In simple words...

Free will means that the decisions I make are genuinely my own.

Like whether I choose tea or coffee...

Whether I study or scroll through my phone...

Are those choices really mine, or were they already decided?

That's the real question.

Keshav

Alright, it's starting to make sense.

But tell me something.

If we really have free will, why does everyone still follow the same basic rules?

It's not like we're completely independent.

We still have laws.

We can't just go around murdering people or do anything we want.

There are so many things we simply can't do.

Nakul

Hold on... you're mixing up two different things. 😄

Free will doesn't mean you can do absolutely anything.

It simply asks this:

Among the choices actually available to you... who is making the choice?

You?

Or was it already decided?

That's the real question.

Keshav

Alright.

Let me ask you something.

When you're watching a movie or a web series, or even reading a novel...

Do you feel the characters are just following a pre-written script?

Or do you feel their choices are actually shaping the story?

Nakul

Honestly...

While I'm watching, it feels like the characters are making their own decisions.

But once it's over, I remember that the script was already written. 😅

That's exactly what confuses me.

What if we're just characters in a story too?

We think we're making choices...

But maybe the script was written long ago. 🤔

Keshav

No, I don't think that's how it works.

We do have free will, and we're not following a fixed script.

Nakul

Then here's another question.

If free will is real...

Can anyone predict my next decision?

Or does God already know what I'm going to choose? 🤔

Keshav

Imagine you're writing a story.

There isn't really a fixed hero or villain.

Instead, you step into one character's shoes and write from their perspective.

Then you do the same with another character...

And then another.

Now tell me...

Who made the decisions in that story?

The author?

Or the characters themselves?

Nakul

Hmm...

From that perspective, it feels like the author made all the decisions.

The only difference is...

The author was thinking as each character.

So to the reader, it feels like the characters made those choices.

Wait...

Are you saying that's how God works too? 🤔

Keshav

No.

What I'm really asking is...

Why do you assume the whole story has already been written?

What if you're writing it as the characters?

Now tell me...

At the end of the story, who should really win?

And by "win," I don't mean the climax.

I mean overall.

If you average out all the joy and suffering...

Who should come out ahead?

Nakul

I'd say the real winner is the one who stayed true to who they were.

Everyone experiences happiness and suffering.

But if, in the end, a character can honestly say,

"I lived my life on my own terms,"

that's a real victory.

Whether they won or lost the final battle doesn't matter that much.

Keshav

Now tell me this.

If you're the one playing every character...

Would you let any of them be treated unfairly?

To you, every character would matter equally.

So if the villain loses in the end, they'll probably have their share of victories before that.

Otherwise, the story would never feel balanced enough to reach its ending.

Nakul

Hmm...

If the author truly sees every character as their own...

That actually makes sense.

Maybe one experiences more joy, another more pain...

But overall, the balance remains.

Still, one question...

If every character belongs to the author...

Why create a villain at all? 😄

Keshav

A villain exists only from the hero's perspective.

If the reader relates to the villain instead...

Then maybe the villain was the hero all along, and the hero was the villain.

Nakul

😂 Fair point.

So maybe "hero" and "villain" are just matters of perspective.

Whoever the reader connects with becomes the hero.

That means the real focus of the story isn't heroes or villains...

It's the journey of every character. 🤔

Keshav

Now let me tell you what I mean by free will.

Do you think any character would willingly choose to compromise?

Obviously not.

So what does the author do?

The author simply adjusts the circumstances to keep the story balanced.

The characters still make their own decisions...

But those decisions don't derail the story.

Everyone has moments of joy and sorrow.

And by the end, everyone's overall satisfaction reaches the same level.

That's how you'd write a story that feels natural.

Nakul

So you're saying...

The characters make their own decisions,

while the author only shapes the circumstances to keep the story balanced.

Interesting...

So God doesn't write our decisions.

He simply arranges the circumstances in a way that keeps the overall story in balance.

Is that what you're saying? 🤔

Keshav

😂 No.

I'm just teaching you how to write a good story, my friend.

Nakul

😂 Hahaha... that's a different thing.

Honestly, from a writer's perspective, I really like that idea.

If you make characters into puppets, the story feels fake.

But if you let them make their own choices and only maintain the balance of the world...

The story feels alive.

Keshav

Alright, now let's get back to your original question.

What exactly were you asking about free will?

Nakul

😄 Right.

My question was simple.

If God already knows what I'm going to choose tomorrow...

Can my choice still be truly free? 🤔

Keshav

Let me ask you something.

If I'm just a reader who's read the same book many times...

Or if I'm the author of the book...

Do the characters still have free will?

And do I already know what's going to happen next?

Nakul

Yeah...

From that perspective, the characters can still have free will within the story.

And I'd know what happens next...

Not because I forced them,

but because I've already seen the whole story.

Keshav

Actually...

Think of it this way.

The author is every character.

Nakul

Oh... now I get it. 😄

So you're saying the author is living through every character.

That's why every decision feels completely natural from the character's point of view...

And yet the author still knows the entire story.

That's an interesting perspective.

Knowing everything and controlling everything aren't necessarily the same thing.

Keshav

Now tell me...

What was your original question again?

Nakul

😂 Right, I almost forgot.

It was simply this...

If God already knows what I'm going to choose...

Is my choice still truly free?

Keshav

I already answered that.

Your decisions have always been yours.

God simply knows them because you're inside the book... while the author holds the entire book in His hands.

Nakul

Hmm...

That's actually a strong analogy.

My decisions are still mine.

God simply sees them because, from His perspective, the whole book is open at once.

So maybe the real issue isn't God's knowledge...

It's the way we experience time.

We're reading one page at a time.

God sees the entire book. 😄

Keshav

Exactly.

Nakul

I guess that's where the confusion comes from.

We assume that if someone already knows what will happen...

They must have forced it to happen.

But that's not necessarily true.

Just because I know how a movie ends...

Doesn't mean the characters acted because of me. 🤔

Nakul

One more question.

If God is the author, and He's living through every character...

Then who's responsible for the mistakes?

Me?

Or God? 🤔

Keshav

No one.

A person's actions simply reflect their nature.

The villain seems evil to us.

But from the villain's own perspective, they're just playing their role.

Nakul

Hmm...

So "good" and "bad" are also matters of perspective.

People simply act according to their nature.

But tell me this...

If someone changes their nature—

Like a thief who becomes an honest man—

Would that change also be an act of free will?

Or was that part of the story too? 🤔

Keshav

If choosing a character was an act of free will...

Then changing the character is also an act of free will, isn't it?

Nakul

😂 Makes sense.

So if my first choice was mine...

Then changing later would also be my own choice.

Keshav

Who said you can rewrite your character whenever you want? 😄

Nakul

😂 Fair enough.

I jumped to that conclusion.

So what you're saying is...

Free will doesn't mean I can change my nature whenever I please.

It simply means I make decisions according to who I truly am.

Did I get that right? 🤔

Keshav

Exactly.

Nakul

Then free will doesn't mean "I can do whatever I want."

It means...

"I choose according to my own nature."

That's a very different definition than the one I started with.

Keshav

Now answer this.

When you read the same book again...

Do the characters suddenly start acting differently?

Nakul

😂 No.

They make the same choices every time...

Because that's simply who they are.

So free will doesn't mean randomness.

It means they genuinely made the choices that naturally arose from their own nature.

Keshav

Exactly.

But that doesn't mean everything is rigidly fixed.

The flow of the story has a direction.

The individual moments don't have to unfold in exactly the same way.

Nakul

Hmm...

I think I get what you're saying.

The events themselves aren't locked in...

But the story still has an overall direction.

Like a river.

The water is always changing...

Yet the river still flows toward the sea.

Interesting.

It doesn't feel completely deterministic...

But it doesn't feel completely random either.

Nakul

One last question. 😄

If the flow is what really matters...

Could the same story have ended differently?

Or no matter how different the path is...

Does the overall balance always remain the same? 🤔

Keshav

Tell me something.

If an actress refuses to do a kissing scene in a movie...

Does the entire story suddenly change?

Does the ending change too?

Nakul

😂 Not necessarily.

The director can rewrite that scene.

Or replace it with something else.

The main story and its ending can still remain the same.

So you're saying...

The details can change...

Without changing the overall direction of the story?

Keshav

Exactly.

Think about it.

Across every Yuga, every Mahāyuga, every Kalpa...

Events continue to unfold.

Different people appear each time.

Yet the overall cycle of the Yugas continues.

The stories aren't copy-pasted...

But the larger pattern remains.

Nakul

That's an interesting way to look at it.

So the pattern repeats...

Not the details.

Every age may have its own struggle between dharma and adharma.

But the people, situations, and choices are never identical.

So God isn't repeating the same story...

Only the rhythm of it. 🤔

Keshav

Take the Ramayana as an example.

In one version, Hanuman is so powerful that he could burn Lanka with a single breath.

In another, Lanka burns because his tail is set on fire.

Either way...

Lanka still burns.

The characters still have free will.

But the story has its own direction.

Nakul

Oh...

Now that makes even more sense.

The overall outcome can remain the same...

Even if the path to it changes.

The characters act according to their own nature.

And somehow the story still finds its way forward.

That's very different from saying everything is mechanically predetermined.

Keshav

So...

Do you think free will exists?

Nakul

😂 After this conversation...

I can't really say "no."

I'd say...

Yes, free will exists.

But free will doesn't mean the story has no direction.

Characters make decisions according to their own nature...

While the story continues to move in its own flow.

I think my mistake was assuming that free will and an overall direction couldn't exist together.

Now it seems they can.

Keshav

This idea reveals more than just the answer to free will.

The more you revisit it...

The more questions it begins to answer from entirely new angles.

Anyway...

See you next time, my friend.

Nakul

😄 I will.

Whether I end up agreeing with it or not...

You've definitely given me a new way to think.

See you, Keshav. 👋
