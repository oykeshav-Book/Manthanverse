Do we really have free will

A conversation between Keshav and Nakul exploring one of philosophy's oldest questions: Are our choices truly free, or are they shaped by causes beyond our awareness?

Characters: Keshav • Nakul

Nakul

Keshav, can I ask you something?

People always say, "Work hard and create your own destiny."

But the same people also say...

"Whatever is meant to happen will happen."

I don't get it.

If everything is already destined, then what's the point of effort?

And if my efforts can truly change everything, then what exactly is destiny?

How can both be true at the same time?

Either destiny is real...

Or free will is.

How can both exist together? 🤔

Keshav

Hey Nakul...

What exactly do you mean by free will? I'm having a hard time connecting with the term.

Nakul

😂 That's exactly what I'm trying to figure out.

In simple words...

Free will means that the decisions I make are genuinely my own.

Like whether I choose tea or coffee...

Whether I study or scroll through my phone...

Are those choices really mine, or were they already decided?

That's the real question.

Keshav

Alright, it's starting to make sense.

But tell me something.

If we really have free will, why does everyone still follow the same basic rules?

It's not like we're completely independent.

We still have laws.

We can't just go around murdering people or do anything we want.

There are so many things we simply can't do.

Nakul

Hold on... you're mixing up two different things. 😄

Free will doesn't mean you can do absolutely anything.

It simply asks this:

Among the choices actually available to you... who is making the choice?

You?

Or was it already decided?

That's the real question.

Keshav

Alright.

Let me ask you something.

When you're watching a movie or a web series, or even reading a novel...

Do you feel the characters are just following a pre-written script?

Or do you feel their choices are actually shaping the story?

Nakul

Honestly...

While I'm watching, it feels like the characters are making their own decisions.

But once it's over, I remember that the script was already written. 😅

That's exactly what confuses me.

What if we're just characters in a story too?

We think we're making choices...

But maybe the script was written long ago. 🤔

Keshav

No, I don't think that's how it works.

We do have free will, and we're not following a fixed script.

Nakul

Then here's another question.

If free will is real...

Can anyone predict my next decision?

Or does God already know what I'm going to choose? 🤔

Keshav

Imagine you're writing a story.

There isn't really a fixed hero or villain.

Instead, you step into one character's shoes and write from their perspective.

Then you do the same with another character...

And then another.

Now tell me...

Who made the decisions in that story?

The author?

Or the characters themselves?

Nakul

Hmm...

From that perspective, it feels like the author made all the decisions.

The only difference is...

The author was thinking as each character.

So to the reader, it feels like the characters made those choices.

Wait...

Are you saying that's how God works too? 🤔

Keshav

No.

What I'm really asking is...

Why do you assume the whole story has already been written?

What if you're writing it as the characters?

Now tell me...

At the end of the story, who should really win?

And by "win," I don't mean the climax.

I mean overall.

If you average out all the joy and suffering...

Who should come out ahead?

Nakul

I'd say the real winner is the one who stayed true to who they were.

Everyone experiences happiness and suffering.

But if, in the end, a character can honestly say,

"I lived my life on my own terms,"

that's a real victory.

Whether they won or lost the final battle doesn't matter that much.
