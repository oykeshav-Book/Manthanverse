The Story of Shikhandi

A timeless story from the Mahabharat

Among the most mysterious and extraordinary characters of the Mahabharata, Shikhandi occupies a unique place.

Shikhandi was not only a great warrior but also a person whose entire life was shaped by an unfinished vow, profound humiliation, and an unwavering quest for justice.

Yet the story of Shikhandi did not begin with their birth.

It began in a previous life.

In that earlier birth, Shikhandi was born as Princess Amba, the eldest daughter of the King of Kashi. She had two younger sisters, Ambika and Ambalika.

When the time came for their marriage, a grand Swayamvara was arranged, attended by kings and princes from many kingdoms.

However, the prince of Hastinapura, Vichitravirya, was not invited.

When Bhishma learned of this, he followed the ancient Kshatriya tradition.

Single-handedly defeating every king present, he placed the three princesses upon his chariot and carried them to Hastinapura so that they could be married to his younger brother, Vichitravirya.

After arriving in Hastinapura, Amba spoke respectfully to Bhishma.

"My heart already belongs to King Shalva. At the Swayamvara, I had intended to choose him as my husband."

Hearing this, Bhishma honored her wishes.

Without hesitation, he respectfully sent Amba to King Shalva.

But an even greater injustice awaited her.

King Shalva refused to accept her.

"Bhishma defeated me in battle and carried you away. I can no longer accept you as my queen."

Amba pleaded with him.

She explained that Bhishma had never touched her and had sent her back with complete honor.

But Shalva refused to listen.

Heartbroken, Amba returned once again to Hastinapura.

She then appealed to Bhishma.

"Now that Shalva has rejected me, you must marry me. It is because of you that I have been left without a place in this world."

Bhishma, however, remembered the terrible vow he had taken long ago.

Having pledged lifelong celibacy, he firmly refused.

Within a single lifetime, Amba had now been rejected twice.

She could neither marry the man she loved nor find acceptance in Hastinapura.

Humiliated, abandoned, and filled with unbearable sorrow, she made a solemn vow.

One day, she would become the cause of Bhishma's downfall.

She sought help from many kings and urged them to challenge Bhishma in battle.

Yet none dared oppose the mighty son of Ganga.

Finally, Amba approached Lord Parashurama, the great sage and the very teacher of Bhishma himself.

She told him the entire story and begged for justice.

Parashurama ordered Bhishma to either marry Amba or fight him in battle.

Bhishma once again chose to uphold his sacred vow and refused to marry.

A terrible battle then erupted between master and disciple.

For many days they fought with unmatched skill.

Neither warrior could defeat the other.

At last, the gods and great sages intervened, bringing the battle to an end.

Yet Amba still received no justice.

Having lost all hope in the world, she turned instead to Lord Shiva.

For many years she performed severe austerities.

Pleased by her unwavering penance, Lord Shiva appeared before her.

Amba asked for only one boon.

"I wish to become the cause of Bhishma's death."

Lord Shiva replied,

"It cannot happen in this lifetime. But in your next birth, you shall indeed become the cause of Bhishma's fall."

Having received this divine promise, Amba realized that the purpose of her present life had come to an end.

She entered the sacred fire and gave up her mortal body.

But her vow remained unfulfilled.

That burning desire for justice would accompany her into another birth.

In time, she would be reborn as Shikhandini, the child of King Drupada of Panchala.

The destiny foretold by Lord Shiva had begun to unfold.
