Who Is Reborn?

A conversation between Advait and Nakul exploring one of humanity's oldest questions: Who is actually reborn? The body, the mind, the soul, or something else entirely?

Characters: Advait • Nakul

Nakul

Hey Advait, do you really believe in reincarnation?

Advait

Yes, I do. Why don't you? By the way, tell me something first—do you think reincarnation happens, or not?

Nakul

I don't believe in it because I haven't seen any scientific evidence for it.

But first, tell me what you mean by reincarnation.

Do you mean that the same soul leaves one body and enters another?

Advait

Do you think the soul is some kind of prisoner trapped inside a body, and after death it simply enters another body?

Nakul

No. That's just the common definition people usually give.

So tell me, what is the soul according to you, and how does reincarnation actually work?

Advait

First, you need to understand something.

The soul, consciousness, or awareness is not something divided into separate pieces.

Suppose I draw a circle. You think the circle is the body and the dot inside it is the soul.

But the actual picture is the opposite.

Imagine a blank page. That blank page is consciousness.

The circle is drawn on that page—that is the body.

Inside that circle are smaller circles, and inside them even smaller circles. Those are just different levels within consciousness.

Do you understand?

Nakul

I think so.

You're saying consciousness isn't a small entity sitting inside the body.

The body exists within consciousness, not the other way around.

Then here's my question:

If consciousness is one and everything exists within it, then what exactly is being "reborn" in reincarnation?

Who is born again?

Advait

Before answering that, let me ask you something.

Science says energy can neither be created nor destroyed—it can only be transformed.

Doesn't science say something similar about information?

Nakul

Energy, yes.

Information is a little more complicated.

In modern physics, especially quantum physics, many scientists believe information may not actually be destroyed, although the subject is still debated.

But why are you asking this? What does it have to do with reincarnation?

Advait

What do you think your identity really is?

Your body?

Or the information that makes you who you are?

If we talk about reincarnation, what exactly should be reborn?

Nakul

From a scientific point of view, my identity isn't just my body.

It's the combination of my memories, experiences, brain structure, personality, and information patterns.

So if reincarnation makes any sense, it would make more sense for information to continue than for the body to continue.

But then comes the real question:

How is that information preserved after death?

Advait

Hinduism often speaks of subtle bodies within the physical body.

Reincarnation belongs to those subtle bodies.

They're not physical.

They're formed by one's karma.

Think of the brain.

Science says the brain doesn't store experiences like a video recording.

It stores raw information, and our mind reconstructs experience from it.

Nakul

So you're saying the subtle body is something like an information structure created by karma, tendencies and impressions?

And you're comparing that to the way the brain stores information?

But then I have a question.

Science always needs a medium to carry information.

Neurons carry brain information.

DNA carries genetic information.

What carries the subtle body if it isn't physical?
