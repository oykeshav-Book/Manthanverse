25 Questions About Hinduism Everyone Should Ask

Questions about karma, dharma, moksha, the Vedas, the Upanishads and the nature of reality.

Advait

The Non-Dualist

Sees the world through the lens of oneness and consciousness. He constantly explores ideas such as Aham Brahmasmi, the nature of the self, and whether all minds are expressions of a single consciousness.

1. What is Hinduism?

Hinduism is a diverse spiritual tradition that explores reality, consciousness, duty, devotion and liberation rather than following a single founder or doctrine.

2. What are the Vedas?

The Vedas are the oldest sacred texts of India, containing hymns, rituals and philosophical insights that shaped Hindu thought.

3. What are the Upanishads?

The Upanishads are philosophical texts that investigate the nature of the self, consciousness and ultimate reality.

4. What is Dharma?

Dharma refers to the principles, responsibilities and way of living that sustain harmony in life and society.

5. What is Karma?

Karma is the idea that actions have consequences and that our choices influence future experiences.

6. What is Moksha?

Moksha is liberation from suffering, ignorance and the cycle of birth and death.

7. What is Samsara?

Samsara is the cycle of birth, death and rebirth through which beings continue until liberation is attained.

8. What is the difference between Atman and Brahman?

Atman is the inner self, while Brahman is the ultimate reality. Many Hindu philosophies teach that they are ultimately one.

9. What does Aham Brahmasmi mean?

It means "I am Brahman" and expresses the insight that the true self is not separate from ultimate reality.

10. Why are there so many gods in Hinduism?

Different deities represent different aspects of the divine, making spiritual ideas accessible through many forms.

11. Who are Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva?

They are associated with creation, preservation and transformation within the cosmic order.

12. What is the Trimurti?

The Trimurti is the concept of Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva as three major expressions of divine activity.

13. Who is Lakshmi?

Lakshmi is associated with prosperity, abundance, beauty and good fortune.

14. Who is Saraswati?

Saraswati represents knowledge, wisdom, learning, music and creativity.

15. Why is Ganesha worshipped first?

Ganesha is traditionally invoked at the beginning of important activities as the remover of obstacles.

16. What is the significance of Krishna?

Krishna is revered as a teacher, friend, guide and divine incarnation who reveals profound spiritual wisdom.

17. What is the Bhagavad Gita?

The Bhagavad Gita is a dialogue on duty, action, devotion and self-realization set within the Mahabharata.

18. Why is the Ramayana important?

The Ramayana explores ideals of duty, devotion, courage and ethical living through the story of Rama.

19. Why is the Mahabharata important?

The Mahabharata examines morality, power, family, justice and the complexity of human choices.

20. What are the Dashavatara?

The Dashavatara are the ten principal incarnations traditionally associated with Vishnu.

21. What is the purpose of meditation?

Meditation is used to cultivate awareness, clarity, inner peace and insight into the nature of the mind.

22. What is Maya?

Maya refers to the appearance of reality that can hide the deeper unity and truth behind existence.

23. Can devotion alone lead to liberation?

Many traditions teach that sincere devotion can become a path to liberation when it transforms the heart and mind.

24. What is the relationship between action and destiny?

Hindu thought generally sees destiny as influenced by past actions while still allowing freedom in the present.

25. What is the ultimate goal of human life?

The highest goal is often described as self-realization, liberation and the direct understanding of one's true nature.

Keshav

The Playful Sage

A practical thinker who mixes wisdom, devotion and humor. Deeply influenced by non-dual ideas, he often jokes about being God himself, yet remains grounded in everyday life and devoted to Lord Rama.

Nakul

The Ordinary Man

Never claims to know the answers and rarely takes philosophy too seriously. Yet his simple questions and sense of humor often reveal what everyone else has overlooked.

Aarav

The Child

Curious, innocent and unafraid to ask what others overlook. He approaches the biggest questions of life with wonder rather than certainty.

Kashi

The Observer

Watches quietly, notices deeply and understands people. Rather than rushing to conclusions, she pays attention to human behavior, emotions and hidden motivations.

Gopi

The Devotee

Sees the world through love, faith and devotion to Krishna. Where others search through logic, she often finds meaning through surrender, trust and devotion.
